# Surface Material State Contract v1

This capability-free seam binds exact climate provenance to the one coarse
material property currently consumed by world generation: dominant surface
reflectance in three bounded spectral bands. Reflectance values are integer
permille fractions of incident radiation.

NIST describes spectrophotometry as quantitative measurement of reflectance
and transmittance and distinguishes bulk optical properties from surface
reflection. USGS surface reflectance likewise measures the fraction of incoming
solar radiation reflected by a surface:

- <https://www.nist.gov/programs-projects/spectrophotometry>
- <https://www.usgs.gov/landsat-missions/landsat-surface-reflectance>

The three bands are a procedural plausibility descriptor, not a measured
spectrum. Composition, chemistry, mixtures, grain structure, roughness, BRDF,
weathering, thermal or mechanical properties, crafting behavior, visibility,
traversability, habitability, biomes, assets and runtime simulation remain out
of scope.
