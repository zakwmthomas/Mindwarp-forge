# G1 C3 Post-Partition Closure Reassessment

Date: 2026-07-15

Status: **C3 remains open; one shared upstream design problem replaces the old
undifferentiated blocker list.**

## Decision

The verified physical-region partition materially changes the C3 closure
assessment. C3 now has bounded unnamed regional structure and already had a
validated environmental-opportunity graph. Those results satisfy the physical
precursors for the master plan's biome and niche obligations at C3's ownership
level. They do not create biome names, ecological roles, organism occupancy or
body plans; those meanings remain C6 work.

C3 is still not closure-ready. Signal propagation, physical visibility and
traversability share missing observer-independent path and occupancy evidence.
They must not be repaired independently with three world-wide scalars. The
next design package must establish one versioned physical substrate from which
channel-specific propagation and probe-specific passage evaluations can be
derived without duplicating geometry or selecting a planet representation.

The second-platform field receipt remains a promotion-quality and G1-closeout
gap, not a reason to keep adding unrelated C3 model breadth. Same-host Python
agreement remains valuable drift protection and must retain its exact
limitations. No qualifying independent platform is installed, and this audit
does not authorize installation.

## Shared-root finding

The three remaining physical gaps are related but not interchangeable:

1. **Common substrate:** exact coordinates or cells, topology, path extent,
   occupied media/materials, surfaces or volumes, boundary behavior and
   reconstruction identity.
2. **Channel-specific propagation:** a declared signal channel and model may
   consume the substrate to produce transfer or attenuation evidence. Optical,
   pressure, chemical, electric and magnetic behavior cannot share one
   unexplained multiplier.
3. **Visibility evaluation:** optical path evidence may be combined later with
   an explicit emitter/receiver query. Biological detector response and
   perception are not environment facts.
4. **Passage evaluation:** the substrate may answer a generic declared probe
   envelope or movement-mode query. Selecting an organism's body envelope,
   locomotion or behavior remains later work.

This split breaks the apparent C3/C6 dependency cycle. C3 can own the physical
facts and generic query contracts without inventing organisms; C6 can later
bind real bodies and senses to those contracts.

## Rejected shortcuts

- **Reuse `SpatialDomain` as terrain or planet geometry:** rejected. Its
  contract is a two-dimensional sampling domain with explicit terrain,
  projection, sphere and world-shape nonclaims.
- **Treat physical-region boundaries as occluders or passage barriers:**
  rejected. A classification boundary proves a signature change, not a wall,
  surface, depth, slope, opacity or clearance constraint.
- **One universal propagation multiplier:** rejected. Required media and
  mechanisms differ by signal channel; the previous caller-authored
  transmission field was removed for this reason.
- **One visibility distance or region score:** rejected. Visibility is a path
  query and lacks geometry, occlusion and channel transfer evidence.
- **One traversability score:** rejected. Passage depends on physical
  continuity plus a declared probe or movement envelope, not a whole-world
  scalar.
- **Move all visibility and traversability work into C6:** rejected. That would
  leave C3 without the environment-owned facts explicitly required by the
  master plan and preserve a circular dependency.
- **Install a VM, container or WSL to unblock this package:** rejected as out of
  authority and unrelated to the missing physical substrate.

## Next design boundary

The next package is a **C3 observer-independent physical path-substrate design
and adversarial review**, not implementation. It must compare at least:

1. enriched two-dimensional cell/edge facts;
2. a bounded 2.5D surface or height-field contract;
3. a bounded three-dimensional occupancy volume; and
4. a topology-agnostic declared graph with explicit physical edge evidence.

The comparison must state which current visibility, propagation and passage
questions each alternative can and cannot answer; exact identity and codec;
finite resource bounds; disconnected and edge behavior; reconstruction and
rollback; compatibility with the current rectified proof domain; and why it
does not imply a sphere, planet, terrain engine, navigation mesh, runtime map
or storage layout.

The design must also compare a shared-substrate architecture against separate
channel-owned geometry and retain the shared form only if it reduces duplicate
truth without becoming a universal cost or path model.

## Stop and authority boundary

Stop before creating a new crate or schema, selecting a geometry alternative,
implementing propagation or attenuation, emitting visibility or
traversability results, defining a body plan, naming a biome, installing a
platform, closing or promoting C3, advancing C4/C6, or selecting runtime
geometry. Those steps require their own reviewed design and authority.
