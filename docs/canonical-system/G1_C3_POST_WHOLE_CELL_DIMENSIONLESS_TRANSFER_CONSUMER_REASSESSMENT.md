## Decision

The implemented `optical-phase-space-dimensionless-transfer` result proves a
strictly narrower fact than brightness or visibility: for one exact band/time
identity and one complete correlated source cell, owner-replayed geometry and
bulk optical depth produce conservative finite, opaque, widened, zero or
unresolved dimensionless transfer evidence while conserving the coupling
owner's exact measure buckets.

This closes the missing same-subject loss prerequisite identified after
receiver coupling. It does not say how much physical quantity the source emits
into that cell, whether the cell's abstract measure has a calibrated physical
unit, how a time-basis identity maps to duration, how multiple source cells or
paths combine, or how any detector responds.

The next bounded action is therefore a mathematical design and counterexample
audit for a subdivision-safe source distribution over the existing optical
phase-space cell measure. Add no production schema or source.

## Required design questions

The next audit must compare at least:

1. absolute conserved quantity assigned to a root cell;
2. density with respect to the existing exact abstract cell measure;
3. a calibrated radiance-like density with explicit area, solid-angle, band
   and duration mappings; and
4. an integral/distribution representation that can remain unresolved without
   inventing a representative sample.

It must state which representation, if any, permits a receiver-bound quantity
enclosure of the form “source distribution integrated over accepted measure,
then bounded by the matching dimensionless transfer” without double-counting
geometric spreading. It must not assume that accepted measure is an aperture,
that normalized parameter measure is steradians or square metres, or that a
nonzero `time_basis_id` supplies a duration.

At minimum the mathematical invariant must bind:

- the exact root and descendant cell identities, ancestry and measure;
- one matching spectral band and explicit time/integration basis;
- parent/child conservation under 4-, 16- and 64-way refinement;
- zero, finite, opaque and unresolved transfer without turning arithmetic
  underflow into physical zero;
- no duplication when accepted and unresolved children coexist;
- coordinate reparameterization and Jacobian counterexamples;
- two sources with equal dimensionless transfer but different quantities;
- one source with positive quantity and zero coupling, and one with zero
  quantity and positive transfer; and
- strict separation of source distribution, receiver-bound physical quantity,
  detector response and perceptual visibility.
