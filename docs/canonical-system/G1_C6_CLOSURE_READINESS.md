## Planning correction: development, heredity and variation

The initial two-fixture body-plan candidate was only an anti-humanoid-universality falsifier. It was not a claim that one humanoid form is sufficient. Review against the complete organism, aging and gameplay records found that the package order introduced applicable variation too late and named reproduction/development only as a hostile rejection. Creature breeding, offspring continuity, inherited traits, families, paired humanoid dimorphism and nonhuman reproductive diversity require positive typed contracts before comparative person-form evaluation.

The body-plan substrate must therefore be sex-neutral and variation-capable from V1. It must separate a shared semantic body-plan family from lawful structural expressions; support typed relation graphs rather than a universal rooted tree; represent homology, conditional or optional structures, cardinality/repetition and declared symmetry as data; and remain coordinate-free. It may not inherit the pelvis root, bilateral assumption, unique-role constraint, coordinates or rest pose of the bounded H2/H3 humanoid representation proof.

Biological sex, reproductive role, sex-determination mechanism, sexual dimorphism, developmental caste/polyphenism, ordinary individual variation, social role, capability, personality and gender/person identity are distinct concepts. No implication may be inferred between them without an explicit species-authored rule and exact evidence. The approved humanoid paired model remains important: masculine and feminine structural envelopes share a homology scaffold while permitting distinct anatomy, proportion and later locomotion/animation calibration. Neither is the universal baseline, and nonhuman lineages do not inherit that binary automatically.

Primary experimental evidence supports keeping these mechanisms explicit rather than encoding one fixed binary switch: genetic and temperature-driven pathways can converge on the same sexual phenotype in one reptile species ([Whiteley et al. 2021](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8049264/)); sequential hermaphroditic fish remodel gonadal tissue across life history ([Ravaglia et al. 2018](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5981258/)); and endocrine/developmental regulation produces caste-specific termite anatomy ([Oguchi and Miura 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10375622/)). These are counterexamples to a universal human-binary schema, not templates to copy into fictional biology.
