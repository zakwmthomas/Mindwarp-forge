# G1 C3 closure-readiness audit

Status: **not closure-ready; next bounded physical-ecology seam selected**.

## Decision

C3 must not be closed by treating every unimplemented layer as a future C6
concern. `MASTER_PLAN_V2.md` explicitly assigns physical visibility,
traversability, biomes and niches to C3 before C4-C6. The current scalar-first
physical chain is credible bounded evidence, but it does not yet satisfy those
named obligations.

This does not require a continuously simulated planet or universe. C3 owns
deterministic physical opportunity and constraint records. C6 owns the
organisms and meanings that occupy or respond to them.

| Master obligation | Current evidence | Closure classification |
|---|---|---|
| stellar through climate conditions | strict bounded replay contracts | bounded prototype evidence; advanced astrophysics, geophysics and weather are explicit non-claims rather than automatic reasons to build a simulator |
| materials and physically caused palette | strict surface-material and derived palette contracts | bounded prototype evidence |
| energy and signals | absorbed-radiation evidence plus strict baseline signal potentials and medium gates | bounded prototype evidence; propagation and detector response remain absent |
| physical visibility | regional exposure changes visible potential, but no geometry, path or atmospheric visibility contract exists | genuine C3 gap; defer until a consumer can state the minimum geometry/path contract |
| traversability | solid-surface fraction exists, but no slope, terrain continuity, fluid depth or body-independent passage constraint exists | genuine C3 gap; do not fabricate body-relative locomotion in C3 |
| biomes | no physical regional envelope or biome partition exists | genuine C3 gap; C3 must provide physical environmental structure before C6 creates biome ecomorphs |
| niches | the current opportunity graph contains signal co-availability only | genuine C3 gap; extend physical opportunities without claiming organism roles, occupancy or anatomy |

## Ownership boundary

C3 may emit only world-caused opportunities and constraints such as available
radiant energy, surface-accessible liquid, atmosphere, solid substrate and
environmental signal channels. These are categorical or normalized bounded
facts derived from already validated contracts.

C6 retains ecological roles, competition, trophic strategies, lineage
occupancy, sensory physiology, body plans, species/ecomorphs, person-form
comparison and population variation. A C3 opportunity never proves that an
organism can exploit it.

## Adversarial findings

- Renaming the signal-only graph a complete niche graph would be false; it has
  no resource, substrate or medium opportunities.
- Adding a universal visibility distance would repeat the removed transmission
  error because path geometry, scattering and observer/detector assumptions
  are absent.
- Adding one traversability score would conflate environmental passage with
  organism morphology and locomotion.
- Requiring advanced orbital, atmospheric or hydrological simulation before
  any C3 progress would exceed the believable-generation goal. Those models
  enter only when a downstream decision needs their missing distinction.
- Second-platform deterministic evidence remains a promotion-quality gap; it
  is not repaired by adding more single-machine model breadth.

## Selected next increment

Reclassify the existing environmental-opportunity graph as a C3 physical
ecology output and extend it from signal-only nodes to a canonical set derived
from the already validated world packet and nested contracts:

- available radiant energy;
- surface-accessible liquid;
- atmosphere;
- solid substrate; and
- supported signal channels.

Co-availability remains only a structural relation. Thresholds are disposable
fixture policy, not biological or scientific constants. The validator must
rebuild the graph from the exact `WorldGenerationInput` and
`CausalWorldPacket`, reject fabricated nodes and preserve deterministic
identity. This is the smallest current-consumer-backed step toward C3's biome
and niche obligations without implementing a universe simulation.

## Stop condition

Stop this increment before habitat suitability, hazards, trophic roles,
organism occupancy, propagation, physical visibility distance, terrain,
body-relative traversability or biome naming. Reassess C3 again after the
expanded physical opportunity graph passes its focused and downstream gates.

## Post-increment route update

The selected opportunity increment, regional exposure/moisture envelope and
exact physical-regime identity now pass. A later consumer audit keeps physical
visibility and body-relative traversability blocked on missing path, terrain
and morphology contracts. The same-host Python receipt also passes, but a
read-only local availability audit found no installed WSL distribution,
container engine, VM, emulator or other qualifying independent platform, so
second-platform replay remains blocked without installation authority.

The next dependency-safe C3 step is therefore a physical-biome readiness audit,
not immediate biome implementation. It must determine whether current
continuous regional evidence and exact-regime equality can support a minimum
observer-independent spatial/partition contract. It must stop before arbitrary
universal thresholds, biome names, habitat suitability, organism roles,
occupancy, aesthetics or runtime simulation. If those semantics require a
content-authored partition recipe or missing terrain/adjacency evidence, the
audit must record that dependency rather than disguise it as a scalar.
