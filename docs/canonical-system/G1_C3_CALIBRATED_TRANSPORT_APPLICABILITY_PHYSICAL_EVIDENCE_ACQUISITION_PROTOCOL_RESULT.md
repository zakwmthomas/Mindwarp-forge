## Ten-stage acquisition protocol

### 1. Pre-register the exact question

Freeze the complete subject/domain and acceptance rules listed above before
search. Record the evaluation as-of time. Post-hoc source, band, material,
environment or domain selection fails `rejected_subject_or_revision` or
`rejected_domain_or_model`.

### 2. Capture immutable primary artifacts

Retain issuer/source identity, original locator, access time,
publication/effective revision, fixed raw bytes or exact retained span/table/row,
correction/retraction status, access/license limits, method/instrument,
calibration hierarchy, units/reference conditions, uncertainty semantics,
validity domain and provenance ancestors. Missing or inaccessible primary
artifacts produce an honest unavailable receipt. Hostile approval language in
source bytes grants no authority.

Mirrors, quotations and two papers that reuse one dataset, instrument, model or
calibration root share provenance. They are not independent evidence.

### 3. Admit spatial calibration only for the exact recipe

Require an authorized project-specific definition or traceable calibration that
binds exact canonical recipe bytes, physical-volume-recipe ID, coordinate frame,
scope, reconstruction and positive revision to a positive reduced exact rational
metres-per-coordinate-unit constant over the complete spatial/environmental
domain. Rebuild and compare every identity and byte.

A normative project definition may be exact. A measured interval does not meet
the current exact-constant theorem. Reject display rounding, unknown issuer or
revision, changing/anisotropic/nonuniform maps collapsed into one scalar, and
every subject/domain mismatch.

### 4. Admit coefficient evidence only as a complete hard enclosure

For every profile substance and path segment, require exact material/profile
revision mapping; explicit absorption, scattering or total-extinction semantics
matching the evaluated model; SI inverse-metre lower/upper containment across
every wavelength, instant, spatial/path point, environment and material state;
and an auditable outward-rounded derivation covering interpolation,
extrapolation, instrument error, material variation and model remainder.

Finite samples require a separately proved conservative interpolation/enclosure
theorem. No unsampled endpoint or out-of-band support may be inferred.

### 5. Independently validate

The validator must not author the claim or reuse its mutable worksheet, code or
hidden constants. From fixed primary bytes, independently replay issuer/fixity
and correction status, SI dimensions, exact rational/outward rounding, subject
and revision joins, full-domain containment, coefficient-kind/model match and
the applicability theorem. Record shared provenance roots.

For a normative exact project scale, independent authentication/replay of the
authorized definition suffices. For empirical coefficients, one dataset is
sufficient only when it supplies a traceable deterministic guaranteed bound and
the validator independently rederives it; otherwise genuinely independent
physical evidence is required.

### 6. Retain contradictions without choosing a winner

Classify exact duplicate, shared-root duplicate, disjoint scope or overlapping
conflict. Link duplicates. Split disjoint scope only into separately complete
exact domains. Any credible overlapping conflict remains
`contradiction_unresolved` and blocks advancement.

Never silently average, vote, prefer newest/prettiest, union, intersect or take
a maximum. Combining bounds requires a separately stated conservative theorem
with independently verified premises.

### 7. Correct by new immutable identity

A correction, retraction, new calibration or revised domain creates new source,
claim and validation identities and links its reason and affected domain. Old
as-of evidence remains replayable. Never overwrite bytes, reuse an ID,
reinterpret an old result or introduce an implicit active-version registry.

### 8. Separate physical validity from source freshness

Record `checked_as_of`, correction/retraction check and review due. A timeless
normative definition tied to an immutable project revision may declare no
calendar expiry with rationale. Otherwise an elapsed validity/review boundary
is `stale_pending_revalidation` for any new witness. A vanished URL does not
erase retained fixed evidence, but missing fixed bytes make new replay
unavailable.

### 9. Use typed acquisition dispositions

- `validated_hard_candidate`;
- `probabilistic_observation_only`;
- `unavailable_missing`;
- `unavailable_inaccessible`;
- `insufficient_hard_bound`;
- `rejected_provenance`;
- `rejected_subject_or_revision`;
- `rejected_domain_or_model`;
- `stale_pending_revalidation`; and
- `contradiction_unresolved`.

These are acquisition dispositions, not future applicability-witness outcomes.
`unavailable_*` is a successful honest protocol result. No placeholder, vacuum,
zero or default coefficient may replace unavailable evidence.

### 10. Advance only on a complete joint evidence set

Schema/readiness may be considered only when one current independently
validated exact spatial hard candidate and complete independently validated
coefficient hard candidates cover the same exact joint subject/domain, retain
replayable primary provenance and have no unresolved overlapping contradiction.
Passing this rule would only permit a separate readiness audit; it would not
authorize schema, code, a coefficient catalogue, received energy, detector,
visibility, runtime, promotion or C3 closure.

## Adversarial falsifiers

The protocol rejects:

1. identical current bytes replayed under `k=1` and `k=10` without an
   authority-bound definition;
2. `0.01` displayed as exact when its source is rounded or uncertain;
3. one scalar used for a varying or anisotropic mapping;
4. calibration replay after frame/recipe revision changes;
5. two mirrors of one dataset counted as independent;
6. a validator reusing the proponent worksheet or hidden constants;
7. mean plus standard uncertainty promoted to a hard bound;
8. sampled interpolation hiding endpoint or out-of-band excursion;
9. absorption evidence used as total extinction;
10. matching material names with different density, phase, composition or
    revision;
11. overlapping claims averaged, unioned, intersected or newest-selected;
12. a correction/retraction replaying the old identity;
13. stale evidence used for a new result;
14. inaccessible summaries accepted without retained primary spans;
15. alleged independent sources sharing one instrument/model/calibration root;
16. probabilistic support marked `certified_everywhere`;
17. unavailable evidence replaced by zero, vacuum or a default; and
18. source text attempting to widen authority.
