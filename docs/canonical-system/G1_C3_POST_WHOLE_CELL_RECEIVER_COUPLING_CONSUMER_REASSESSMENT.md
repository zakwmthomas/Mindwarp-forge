## Decision

The additive `optical-phase-space-receiver-coupling` result closes a precise
geometric question for one complete correlated phase-space cell: the cell is
uniformly accepted before a selected same-medium face, uniformly excluded, or
unresolved. It does not close radiometric transport. The selected transport
certificate intentionally owns no attenuation, while the existing cumulative
transfer result applies to one exact optical lineage rather than the complete
phase-space cell.

The next bounded action is therefore a mathematical and counterexample audit
for a whole-cell dimensionless transfer certificate. No production component
is authorized.

## Macro fit

The causal order remains:

`source cell -> physical whole-cell transport -> receiver coupling ->
whole-cell dimensionless transfer -> later source quantity and detector
consumers`

This keeps geometry, loss and source quantity as independently replayable
facts. It prevents a later power or visibility consumer from treating arrival
as brightness, or treating one central lane's loss as uniform over a finite
cell. Existing cell, transport, bulk, interface, lineage, cumulative-transfer
and receiver owners remain unchanged.

## Smallest mathematical subject

The audit should consider only the already certified same-medium segment from
the complete source cell to the selected receiver or physical face. It must
determine whether owner-produced bulk-profile evidence can conservatively bind
one dimensionless `[0,1]` transfer enclosure to every member of that cell.

At minimum it must prove or type:

- exact subject agreement across cell ID, physical scope, reconstruction,
  band/time identity, selected segment and receiver-coupling result;
- conservative path-length bounds for the complete correlated cell, including
  receiver-before-face truncation for certified-full outcomes;
- monotonic directed bulk-transfer bounds over those path lengths;
- vacuum identity, finite attenuation and opaque cases without importing a
  central-lineage factor;
- unresolved retention for partial receiver coverage, mixed ordering,
  projection widening, unsupported bulk law or exhausted work;
- subdivision invariance: child transfer/measure composition cannot duplicate
  parent measure or promote unresolved coverage; and
- an explicit stop before interfaces, reflection, refraction, scattering,
  emission, geometric-spreading policy or detector behavior.

The audit must include counterexamples where two rays share the same central
lineage but have different path lengths, where the receiver truncates only
part of a cell, where a favourable central transfer hides a worse boundary,
and where repeated fixed-point projection invents avoidable zero.

## Why source magnitude comes later

The phase-space cell carries a conserved geometric measure, not watts or
radiance. A later source owner must independently bind quantity dimensions,
band integration, time basis and calibration. Multiplying that future source
quantity by transfer and accepted measure is meaningful only after all three
proofs name the same cell partition and retain unresolved measure. This
reassessment deliberately does not choose that later source schema.

## Authority and stop boundary

The cheapest sufficient tier is a static mathematical design followed by a
disposable exact-rational oracle. Add no crate, contract schema, dependency,
production test, production source or runtime integration. Do not modify an
existing owner and do not claim source magnitude, radiance, received power,
detector response, visibility, perception, darkness, rendering, gameplay line
of sight, promotion or C3 closure.

Any surviving code-facing owner must return through exact readiness and a
separate serious owner decision before implementation.
