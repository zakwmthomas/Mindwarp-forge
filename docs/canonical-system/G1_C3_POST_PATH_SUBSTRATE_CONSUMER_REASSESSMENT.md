# G1 C3 Post-Path-Substrate Consumer Reassessment

Date: 2026-07-15

## Decision

The verified `physical-path-substrate` closes the shared geometry and occupancy
dependency for physical propagation and generic passage queries. It does not
close either consumer. The safe route is two separate typed design packages
that reuse the same exact path witness and substance identities:

1. a visible-radiance path-transfer mathematical design audit; then
2. a generic probe/contact-and-passage design audit.

They must not share a universal transfer, resistance, cost or material score.
Their only shared truth is the reconstructed volume, exact path/contact
evidence and exact substance identity. C3 remains executing.

## What the substrate now proves

The substrate can reconstruct a finite, bounded-absent 3D occupancy volume and
return exact closed-cell interval and contact witnesses for a declared path. It
distinguishes unavailable evidence, vacuum, gas, liquid and solid occupancy,
and binds every non-vacuum cell to a substance identity.

That is enough to identify *where* a path passes and *what substance evidence*
it encounters. It deliberately does not say how a channel interacts with that
substance, whether a probe can occupy or cross it, or what an organism can see
or traverse.

## Failure points engineered out

| Tempting shortcut | Counterexample | Required boundary |
|---|---|---|
| `solid = opaque` | transparent glass is solid; an opaque aerosol need not be | visible-radiance interaction evidence is typed by channel and substance |
| gas/liquid imply one attenuation | vacuum, clear air, fog and water have different channel behavior | participating-medium transfer uses declared coefficients and units |
| multiply once per occupied cell | changing cell resolution changes the result | transfer depends on physical path length and declared interfaces, not cell count |
| `solid = impassable` or supportive | a declared probe may pass a mesh or fail against fluid pressure; ice and mud differ mechanically | passage uses typed probe/contact evidence |
| one world traversability score | passage depends on probe envelope, clearance, support and movement/contact mode | return evidence for a declared generic probe, not a universal ranking |
| one universal substance property bag | optics and mechanics evolve under different units, provenance and validation rules | use separate typed interaction profiles keyed by the same substance ID |
| biome/region category drives presentation | categorical ownership creates visible seams unrelated to physical causes | continuous causal fields produce deterministic ecotones; only sharp physical causes produce sharp boundaries |

Strict unavailable evidence must remain unavailable in both branches. It
cannot silently become transparent, opaque, passable or blocked. Point contacts
and simultaneous boundary contacts also require explicit consumer rules; the
substrate must not invent their optical or mechanical meaning.

## Package 1: visible-radiance path-transfer mathematical design audit

This is the exact next C3 package. It is design-only and must determine:

- a typed visible-radiance channel/profile contract and the authority that
  supplies substance interaction evidence;
- resolution-independent transfer over exact witness intervals using declared
  physical units;
- separate treatment of participating media and surface/interface events;
- deterministic fixed numerical policy for geometric length and transfer,
  including the square-root and exponential/approximation problem;
- endpoint, point-contact, simultaneous-interface, stationary-path and
  unavailable-evidence semantics;
- canonical provenance, reconstruction, hostile counterexamples and cost
  ceilings.

The audit must compare and reject binary solid occlusion and per-cell universal
multipliers unless a narrower typed case proves them from explicit evidence.
“Visibility” in this package means observer-independent visible-radiance
transfer opportunity. Biological perception, eyes, receiver sensitivity,
detectability and presentation remain consumer-owned.

## Package 2: generic probe/contact-and-passage design audit

This package follows rather than being folded into optics. It must define a
declared generic probe envelope and typed mechanical interaction evidence for
clearance, contact, support and passage. It must address phase/substance
behavior, unavailable evidence, boundary contacts, path versus swept-volume
needs and deterministic evidence output.

It may not infer an organism, body plan, gait, navigation policy or destination
fitness. C6 may later bind organism-specific bodies and movement modes to the
generic evidence without changing C3 occupancy truth.

## Dependency and authority result

No shared consumer contract beyond exact substrate evidence is justified. A
future source may publish multiple typed interaction profiles keyed by one
substance identity, but no untyped catch-all property bag or universal score is
authorized.

The visible-radiance mathematical audit comes first because its straight-path
input is already supported and because its unresolved deterministic length and
transfer arithmetic can be falsified without inventing a body envelope. The
probe package has an additional swept-volume/contact-model decision and must
remain separate.

This reassessment authorizes no consumer implementation, material coefficient,
probe shape, sphere, planet, terrain, navigation/runtime system, biological
visibility claim, C3 closure or promotion.

## Biome continuity result

Nothing in either route changes the biome rule. Exact cells, substances and
physical regions are evidence boundaries, not automatic visible biome
boundaries. Biome materials and palettes must blend through continuous causal
fields where the underlying causes vary continuously. A sharp transition is
retained only when supported by a sharp physical cause.
