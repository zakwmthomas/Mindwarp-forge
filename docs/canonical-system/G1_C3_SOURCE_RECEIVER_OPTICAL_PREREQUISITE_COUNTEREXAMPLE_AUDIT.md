## Counterexamples

### A terminal face is not a receiver

Two manifests can have byte-identical local transfer factors and final hit
boxes while only one external receiver contains that hit. Because the manifest
binds no receiver, labelling either as arrived is ambient evidence. Outer exit,
work exhaustion, unavailable evidence and interface termination are likewise
not receiver outcomes.

### Receiver containment cannot be reconstructed from a cell label

A receiver smaller than a cell may lie before the next face, after the certified
hit, tangent to the conditional lane, or outside the direction box. Reusing the
current-cell index as receiver containment accepts all four incompatible cases.
A future arrival route therefore needs separate receiver geometry and exact
receiver-before-face ordering evidence; it is not a field added to the current
manifest.

### Cross-band multiplication fabricates a path

Red, green and blue may take different interface branches and later cells. An
RGB aggregate that multiplies red bulk evidence by green transmitted power can
look numerically bounded while describing no lane. A cumulative record must
bind exactly one manifest band and every selected factor identity.

### Repeated Q0.48 projection can invent avoidable zero

Multiplying and projecting to Q0.48 after every factor can drive a small
positive enclosure to `[0,0]` even when the exact rational product is positive.
That is avoidable rounding loss, not opacity or darkness. The design audit must
compare a wider retained accumulator against exact rational oracle bounds and
must retain underflow as an arithmetic/certification distinction rather than a
perceptual classification.

### Interface terminal power is not a followed factor

For `all_tir`, ambiguous branch, nonconvergence and unsupported model, the
lineage stops at the incident side. Multiplying reflected, guessed transmitted
or zero power into a forward cumulative result would silently choose an
unfollowed branch. Only an owner-validated `continue_after_interface` step may
contribute that selected band's transmitted factor.

### Zero transfer is not visibility or darkness

An opaque bulk factor can make the direct-beam product exactly zero while
scattering, emission, other bands, other paths and detector behavior remain
unmodelled. Conversely a positive upper bound does not prove arrival or
detectability. The output must remain a dimensionless followed-lane transfer
enclosure with no receiver, perception or rendering meaning.

## Selected mathematical seam

The next audit may design, but not implement, one capability-free cumulative
lane-transfer owner downstream of `optical-lineage-binding`. It may replay the
complete bundle and manifest, select only:

- one bulk `VacuumIdentity`, finite Q0.48 enclosure or opaque zero factor for
  each retained step; and
- one same-band transmitted Q0.48 enclosure only for each
  `continue_after_interface` disposition.

It must compare exact rational multiplication with a bounded directed
fixed-width accumulator, freeze factor ordering and identity domains, preserve
all ten lineage terminals, and return both the cumulative enclosure to the
terminal boundary and an explicit statement that no receiver arrival was
evaluated. It must not reinterpret unavailable or ambiguous evidence as zero.

At minimum, the hostile portfolio must include factor deletion, duplication,
reordering, cross-band substitution, stale manifest, independently resealed
local factor, terminal-interface factor injection, zero-to-positive mutation,
positive-to-zero rounding, accumulator overflow, cap bypass and
authority/limitation mutation.

## Why this step precedes receiver work

It uses only quantities already produced by verified local owners, has a clear
dimensionless invariant and can be falsified independently. Receiver arrival
still requires a later geometry contract, but designing the cumulative fold
now neither fabricates that geometry nor blocks it. A later source-to-receiver
record may compose the two independent proofs rather than owning both at once.
