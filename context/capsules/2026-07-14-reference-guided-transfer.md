## Owner direction that must survive transfer

1. The previous game project was rebuilt from the ground up because its broad,
   multi-program, category-first, scaffold-heavy approach did not produce a
   trustworthy foundation.
2. Old and external assets must be treated as **references and test targets**,
   not imported foundations, canonical baselines, or architectural authority.
3. In Forge terminology, a reference is an example used to understand or
   measure a desired result. A baseline is Forge's own current output before an
   improvement experiment.
4. A named animal may demonstrate one possible expression of functional roles,
   but its name or mesh must not become a generator category. Semantic
   extraction must separate directly visible structural observations from
   behavioural or ecological claims that need independent evidence.
5. Humanoids need stricter reference-guided convergence. The Forge should build
   its own humanoids and compare them with varied licensed targets across
   proportions, landmarks, silhouette, occupied volume, mass distribution,
   surface coverage, skeleton, articulation, deformation, motion, and
   controlled visual review.
6. A single 90-95 percent score is unsafe. Critical dimensions remain separate,
   and a severe local defect cannot be hidden by a good average.
7. Voxel or occupancy representations may help compare mass and volume, but no
   final mesh, voxel, surface, field, assembly, or hybrid representation has
   been selected.
8. The current T-pose work is a proof of rest-pose, hierarchy, proportion, and
   comparison semantics. It is not an approved humanoid or animation baseline.
9. No external software installation, engine selection, production promotion,
   protected-Kernel mutation, or automatic owner judgment is authorized.

## Pending plan

The latest proposed plan is **Reference-Guided Generation Without Copying the
References**. It supersedes the earlier proposal that described external human
and creature material as baselines. The complete pending plan is included in
the portable handover export as `REFERENCE_GUIDED_GENERATION_PLAN.md`.

The proposal introduces only evidence-side concepts around the existing Forge
records:

- `ReferenceTarget`: provenance, permitted use, direct claims, limitations, and
  forbidden uses;
- `ReferenceObservation`: one extracted claim with method, evidence, and
  uncertainty;
- `ReferenceSuite`: multiple targets used to test a capability without letting
  one example dominate;
- `TargetComparison`: normalization plus separate structural and perceptual
  results; and
- `ReferenceGeneralisationResult`: withheld-target and diversity evidence.

These are proposal details, not approved interfaces. Existing representation,
experiment, decision, lineage, review, rollback, and transfer records remain
the current authority.
